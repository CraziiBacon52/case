How To's

Using resources such as Zoom, E-Mail, and more tech Vocabulary

What is Zoom?

Zoom is a tool that is used for video conferencing. This allows large groups to see each other through a video, communicate, and share screens all from their homes.

Signing Up for Zoom

Step 1: Go to zoom.us/signup

Step 2: You will be asked to enter your birthdate. Unless you're older than 16, you're not eligible to create an account unless it's for school.

Step 3: It will then present you with options to sign up. Enter your work/school email (personal works fine as well)

Step 4: You could also create an account by pressing 'sign in with (Google, Facebook)' If you choose this option, skip to step 8

Step 5: If you entered your e-mail address, zoom will send an e-mail to your account. Click the button that says 'activate account'

Step 6: A page will open in your web browser, asking if you're signing up on behalf of school. Answer yes or no.

Step 7: From there, fill in your last name and desired password for Zoom.

Step 8: You'll be asked if you want to invite other people to join Zoom. You can either skip over that page, or invite others.

Step 9: Finally, you'll be given your personal meeting URL and will have the option to click a button that says 'start meeting now'. If you click the button, you'll be prompted to download the Zoom Desktop App.

Congrats! You're signed up!

Go to zoom.us/signup

Downloading Zoom

Step 1: Go to zoom.us/download

Step 2: This will take you to a page with download options. Click the download button under 'Zoom Client for Meetings'

Step 3: If you click on the download on the bottom of your webpage, you'll open a popup window. Click 'continue', the destination (usually 'for this user only'), then 'install'

Congrats! You've installed Zoom

Go to zoom.us/download

Hosting a Meeting

Hosting a Zoom meeting is possible if you have the app downloaded. If you don't, go to the section above and follow the steps.

Step 1: Open up the app on your computer and sign into your account.

Step 2: Click the downward arrow on the 'start meeting' section. (It's an orange button)

Step 3: Check 'start with video' and 'use my personal meeting id' and then send the id to those that you want in the Zoom.

That's it!

Joining a Meeting

When joining a meeting, the host will most likely send you either a link or a code

Step 1a (link): When it is time to join the call, click the link.

Step 2a (link): You will either automatically join the call or be placed in a waiting room, where you wait for the host to let you in.

Step 1b (code): If they send you a code, then open the zoom app.

Step 2b (code): Click the button that says 'join meeting' and input the code that was given to you. You can also type your name in which will be displayed as you join the call.

Turning Video and Microphone On and Off

On the bottom of your screen on a call, there are multiple options. Two of them are the microphone and video. One is a picture of a microphone and the other is a camera the says 'video' underneath it.

To turn off your video, click the button that says video. This way, no one can see you. If you want to turn it back on, click it again.

So that people can't hear you, click the microphone button. To turn it back on, click it again.

Extra Functions

Face Touch-up

Step 1: Open the Zoom app and click the little gear in the top right corner (settings)

A screen will open up with different options. Click the section titled 'video'. These are settings that will edit the video of you that others see.

One useful function of this section is that you get a preview of your video. This can be good to look at before you join a call. In the image where there is a cartoon is where your video will appear.

Under the section entitled 'my video', you can click 'touch up my appearance'. This simply smooths out your face by retouching your video.

Recording Meetings

Once you are in a meeting, there is an option on the bottom of the screen that says 'record'

Once you press this button, it will start recording everything that is happening in the meeting.

In the top left corner, there is an option that will allow you to pause/stop and restart the video. All you have to do is press the pause/play symbol.

After the call, the video will automatically download onto your computer, and it is also visible in the 'recordings' tab of your zoom app.

Sharing Screens

On the bottom there is an option that says 'share screen'. If you are the host, you'll automatically have the option. If not, the host has to give you permission to share your screen.

Press the button that says 'share screen'. The host will be offered options, such as only enabling one person to share the screen, etc.

Next, a window will open asking what you want to share. Some of the options are your desktop, apps that you have open (such as Chrome, Safari), a whiteboard, etc. Click one of these.

Once you've chosen what you want to share, click the button that says 'share' on the bottom right corner.

To start a screen share, type ⌘Cmd+Shift+S (PC: Alt+Shift+S).

To pause/resume a screen share, type ⌘Cmd+Shift+T (PC: Alt+T).
