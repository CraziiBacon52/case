About & Donate

How is COVID-19 affecting learning in lower-income and less tech-savvy families? What are we doing to help?

What is Happening?

Due to the rapid spread of COVID-19, schools have been shut down and forced to revert to distance learning, meaning that students do all of their work exclusively online. Most schools are utilizing services such as Zoom, which allow students and teachers participate in video conferences. Some students have no issues participating, but lower-income and less tech-savvy families are disproportionately impacted. The goal of this website is to raise awareness about certain nonprofits that are helping those impacted and also to provide guidance on how to use certain resources such as Zoom, e-mail, and applications provided by Google.

Supporting Initiatives

One nonprofit, Silicon Valley Strong, has an initiative called the Digital Inclusion fund. Donations provide computers and internet access for lower-income students in the Silicon Valley area. Another nonprofit is Silicon Valley Community Foundation, which has created the COVID-19 Education Partnership. They provide IT support for students and staff, family engagement strategies, and more!
